## CRITICAL SEVERITY

### 1. Concatenated PubMed/Crossref Labels in Bibliography (C-012 / C-023)

- **Severity:** CRITICAL
- **Source:** Converter
- **Scope:** Systemic (1,898 instances across 79 reference files)
- **Known Issue ID:** C-012 / C-023
- **Description:** PubMed, Crossref, and PubMed Central labels in bibliography entries are concatenated without separators. They also appear as plain text instead of hyperlinks, losing the source URLs.
- **Example:**
  - Source: `<a href="https://doi.org/10.1016/...">Crossref</a><a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/...">PubMed</a>`
  - Converted: `CrossrefPubMed` (plain text, no links)
  - Rendered: "CrossrefPubMed" — unclickable, labels mashed together
- **Worst affected files:** ch0018s0010 (217 instances), ch0041s0014 (96 instances), ch0037s0010 (84 instances)

### 2. Double Periods Before DOI Links (C-006)

- **Severity:** CRITICAL
- **Source:** Converter
- **Scope:** Systemic (900 `.. https` instances across 41 reference files)
- **Known Issue ID:** C-006
- **Description:** Bibliography entries have double periods (`..`) immediately before DOI URLs. This occurs when the converter appends a period after text that already ends with one.
- **Example:**
  - Rendered: `2019;85(5):504-12.. https://doi.org/10.1002/...`
  - Expected: `2019;85(5):504-12. https://doi.org/10.1002/...`

### 3. Missing Spaces Around Inline Emphasis (C-011)

- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Source:** Converter
- **Scope:** Systemic (127+ files with missing space before `<emphasis>`, 14+ files with missing space after `</emphasis>`)
- **Known Issue ID:** C-011
- **Description:** Words run into bold/italic text without whitespace separation. This affects readability of medical terminology where precise formatting matters.
- **Examples observed in browser:**
  - `releasedthe` (ch0009s0004) — "released" + "the" concatenated
  - `changingPCA/NCA` (ch0059s0008) — "changing" + "PCA/NCA" concatenated
- **Impact:** Medical terms and body text merge, making content harder to read

### 4. Period-Space in DOI URLs and Abbreviations (C-013)

- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Source:** Converter
- **Scope:** Systemic (87 "doi. org" instances across 87 reference files; 6+ "e. g." instances in body text)
- **Known Issue ID:** C-013
- **Description:** Post-processing inserts spurious spaces after periods, breaking DOI URLs and splitting abbreviations.
- **Examples:**
  - `doi. org` instead of `doi.org` (87 reference files)
  - `e. g.` instead of `e.g.` (ch0080s0008, ch0048s0005, ch0035s0006, etc.)
- **Impact:** DOI links may break; abbreviations look incorrect

### 5. ContactOf Author Markers in Copyright Blocks (C-014)

- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Source:** Converter
- **Scope:** Systemic (154 instances across 78 files)
- **Known Issue ID:** C-014
- **Description:** Raw "ContactOf Author" CSS class names appear as visible text in copyright/metadata blocks instead of being suppressed or converted to appropriate formatting.
- **Example:**
  - Converted: `<superscript>1, 2 <emphasis>ContactOf Author 1</emphasis>`
  - Rendered: "1, 2 ContactOf Author 1" — meaningless to the reader

### 6. Ordered List Misclassification (C-017)

- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Source:** Converter
- **Scope:** Systemic (617 source `<ol>` elements → only 107 `<orderedlist>` in converted; 120 files have `<itemizedlist mark="none">`)
- **Known Issue ID:** C-017
- **Description:** The converter incorrectly classifies many ordered lists (`<ol>`) as `<itemizedlist mark="none">` when it detects pre-numbered content. This changes numbered lists to unstyled bullet-less lists, losing the numbered sequence.
- **Impact:** Lists that should show numbered steps (e.g., surgical protocols, treatment steps) render without numbering

## MEDIUM SEVERITY

### 7. Keywords Without Separators (C-026 — NEW)

- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Source:** Converter
- **Scope:** Systemic (~86 keyword sections across the book)
- **Known Issue ID:** C-026 (NEW)
- **Description:** Keywords are concatenated without commas or separators. Source XHTML has keywords in individual `<span class="Keyword">` elements, but the converter joins them into a single text string.
- **Example:**
  - Source: `<span class="Keyword">Epidemiology childhood cancer</span>` `<span class="Keyword">Demography of cancer</span>` (separate spans)
  - Converted: `<para>Epidemiology childhood cancer Demography of cancer Global cancer Global childhood cancer burden</para>`
  - Expected: `Epidemiology childhood cancer, Demography of cancer, Global cancer, Global childhood cancer burden`
