## CRITICAL SEVERITY

### 1. Concatenated PubMed/Crossref Labels in Bibliography (C-012 / C-023)

- **Severity:** CRITICAL
- **Source:** Converter
- **Scope:** Systemic (References section ch0018s0001 — all entries with external links)
- **Known Issue ID:** C-012 / C-023
- **Description:** PubMed, Crossref, and PubMed Central labels in bibliography entries are concatenated without separators. They also appear as plain text instead of hyperlinks, losing the source URLs.
- **Examples observed:**
  - Entry [1]: `CrossrefPubMed` (plain text, no links)
  - Entry [2]: `CrossrefPubMedPubMed Central` (triple concatenation)
  - Entry [3]: `CrossrefPubMedPubMed Central`
  - Entry [5]: `CrossrefPubMedPubMed Central`
- **Impact:** Reference links are unusable — researchers cannot click through to PubMed/Crossref sources

### 2. Double Periods in References (C-006)

- **Severity:** CRITICAL
- **Source:** Converter
- **Scope:** Systemic (References section ch0018s0001)
- **Known Issue ID:** C-006
- **Description:** Bibliography entries have double periods (`..`) in citation text, typically after page ranges.
- **Example:**
  - Entry [4]: `796–807.. Epub 2020 May 13.`
  - Expected: `796–807. Epub 2020 May 13.`

### 4. Missing Spaces Around Inline Emphasis (C-011)

- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Source:** Converter
- **Scope:** Systemic (multiple instances per chapter across the book)
- **Known Issue ID:** C-011
- **Description:** Words run into bold/italic text without whitespace separation. Particularly severe in this book — found 6+ instances in a single section (ch0010s0003).
- **Examples observed in browser:**
  - `withoutdefining` — "without" + "defining" (ch0010s0003)
  - `andit` — "and" + "it" (ch0010s0003)
  - `temporalmechanisms` — "temporal" + "mechanisms" (ch0010s0003)
  - `considerreal` — "consider" + "real" (ch0010s0003)
  - `processesin` — "processes" + "in" (ch0010s0003)
  - `withrestricted` — "with" + "restricted" (ch0010s0003)
  - `ofprecision` — "of" + "precision" (ch0014s0010)
  - `abeneficial` — "a" + "beneficial" (ch0014s0015)
- **Impact:** Medical and scientific terms merge with surrounding text, severely impacting readability

### 5. Period-Space in DOI URLs and Abbreviations (C-013)

- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Source:** Converter
- **Scope:** Systemic (DOI URLs in chapter title sections + abbreviations in body text)
- **Known Issue ID:** C-013
- **Description:** Post-processing inserts spurious spaces after periods, breaking DOI URLs and splitting abbreviations.
- **Examples:**
  - `https://doi. org/10.1007/978-3-032-08808-6_1` — chapter title sections (all 15 chapters)
  - `dop9054@med. cornell. edu` — email address in chapter title sections
  - `e. g.` instead of `e.g.` (ch0014s0020)
  - `i. e.` instead of `i.e.` (ch0014s0010)
- **Impact:** DOI links broken; abbreviations look incorrect; email addresses malformed

### 6. ContactOf Author Markers in Copyright Blocks (C-014)

- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Source:** Converter
- **Scope:** Systemic (all 15 chapter title sections)
- **Known Issue ID:** C-014
- **Description:** Raw "ContactOf Author" CSS class names appear as visible superscript text in chapter copyright/metadata blocks instead of being suppressed.
- **Example:**
  - Rendered: `Doru Paul¹ ContactOf Author 2(1)` — meaningless to the reader
- **Impact:** Stray metadata markers appear in every chapter's title section
