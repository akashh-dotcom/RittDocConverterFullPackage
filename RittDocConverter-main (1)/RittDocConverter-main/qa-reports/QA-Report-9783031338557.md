## MEDIUM SEVERITY

### 1. Duplicate Ordered List Numbering (C-024)

- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Source:** Converter
- **Scope:** Systemic (affects all chapters with ordered lists)
- **Known Issue ID:** C-024
- **Description:** Ordered lists show duplicate numbering — platform auto-numbering ("1.", "2.", "3.") plus text-embedded numbers that should have been stripped.
- **Example:**
  - **Location:** ch0003s0001 (Ch 3 Keywords — "Check Your Knowledge and Experience")
  - **Rendered:** "1. 1. What are the most common direct haematological complications of cancer?"
  - **Expected:** "1. What are the most common direct haematological complications of cancer?"
- **Root Cause:** The converter creates `<orderedlist>` elements but the postprocessor's `process_ordered_lists()` did not strip the leading numbers from the text content. This book was converted before the B2 code fix was applied, or the XML fix script was not run on this book's output.

### 2. Period-Space Inserted in Email Addresses (C-013 variant)

- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Source:** Converter
- **Scope:** Systemic (affects all chapter landing pages with author email addresses)
- **Known Issue ID:** C-013
- **Description:** The `NO_SPACE_AFTER_PUNCT` regex in the postprocessor inserts spaces after periods in email addresses displayed on chapter landing pages. Email domain names are broken by spaces.
- **Examples:**
  - **Ch 9 landing page:** `lucy. Grant@berkshire. nhs. uk` (should be `lucy.Grant@berkshire.nhs.uk`)
  - **Ch 9 landing page:** `mark. foulkes@royalberkshire. nhs. uk` (should be `mark.foulkes@royalberkshire.nhs.uk`)
- **Root Cause:** The spacing normalization regex `\.([A-Z])` → `. \1` fires on `Grant` (uppercase G after period), and `NO_SPACE_AFTER_PUNCT` likely does not exclude content inside `<ulink>` elements. The lowercase-after-period cases (`nhs. uk`) suggest an additional regex or the `NO_SPACE_AFTER_PUNCT` pattern is broader than `\.([A-Z])` in the version used for this book.

### 3. Stray "ContactOfAuthor" Markers on Chapter Landing Pages (C-014)

- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Source:** Converter
- **Scope:** Systemic (all chapter landing pages)
- **Known Issue ID:** C-014
- **Description:** Chapter landing pages display stray italic text "ContactOfAuthor 1", "ContactOfAuthor 2" after author names, and stray "(1)", "(2)" affiliation numbers that should be suppressed or rendered differently.
- **Examples:**
  - **Ch 9:** `Lucy Grant¹ ContactOfAuthor 1 and Mark Foulkes² ContactOfAuthor 2(1)`
  - **Ch 3:** `Karen Sayal¹ (1)`
- **Root Cause:** The converter is rendering the `ContactOfAuthor` span class name as visible italic text instead of suppressing it or converting it to a mailto link marker. The `(1)`, `(2)` affiliation numbers in parentheses are also being output as visible text.

## LOW SEVERITY

### 4. Metadata Concatenation on Chapter Landing Pages

- **Severity:** LOW
- **Source:** Converter
- **Scope:** Systemic
- **Known Issue ID:** C-014 (related)
- **Description:** Author affiliation text and next author name run together without proper line breaks on chapter landing pages.
- **Examples:**
  - **Ch 9:** `Reading, UKLucy Grant Email:` — "UK" runs directly into "Lucy Grant" with no line break
  - **Ch 9:** `(Corresponding author)Email:` — no space between ")" and "Email:"
  - **pr0003 (Copyright):** `Carlisle, UK Prof Andreas Charalambous` — missing line break between affiliation and next author

### 5. CC BY Logo Instead of Book Cover (R-004)

- **Severity:** LOW
- **Source:** Platform
- **Scope:** Isolated (pr0001, pr0002)
- **Known Issue ID:** R-004
- **Description:** Front matter pages pr0001 and pr0002 display the Creative Commons CC BY license logo instead of the actual book cover image. This is a known platform issue for Open Access books.

### 6. Keywords Displayed as Unseparated String

- **Severity:** LOW
- **Source:** Converter
- **Scope:** Systemic (affects all chapters with keywords sections)
- **Known Issue ID:** NEW (C-026)
- **Description:** Keywords on the Keywords section pages are displayed as a continuous string without separators (commas, semicolons, or line breaks).
- **Example:**
  - **Ch 3 (ch0003s0001):** `COVID-19 Cancer care Thromboembolic events Cytokine storm Neutropenic sepsis Patient well-being`
  - **Expected:** `COVID-19, Cancer care, Thromboembolic events, Cytokine storm, Neutropenic sepsis, Patient well-being`
